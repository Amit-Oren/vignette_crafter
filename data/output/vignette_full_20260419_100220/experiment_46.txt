**Case Vignette: Kai**

Kai, a 23-year-old non-binary European individual, presents with significant distress following a
terrorist attack they survived two years ago, which also precipitated the breakdown of their
marriage. Their life is now dominated by a state of high alert. Unpredictable situations, like a
sudden change in a train schedule, instantly trigger a surge of anxiety and physical reactivity,
their heart pounding as if in immediate danger. This hypervigilance makes concentration nearly
impossible, leaving them feeling perpetually on guard and exhausted.

The trauma is not a coherent narrative for Kai but a series of terrifying, frozen snapshots. When a
loud shout echoes in the street, it doesn't just startle them; it catapults them directly back into
the event. They vividly relive those moments, feeling the chaos happening *now*, and sometimes hear
the specific, terrifying sounds from that day echoing in their mind. These intrusive memories are
profoundly disorienting and distressing.

Kai has come to interpret these experiences through a lens of permanent, personal damage. They feel
they have lost the happy person they once were and are now irrevocably miserable. A core belief has
solidified: there is something fundamentally wrong with them as a person, and because of this,
nothing good can possibly lie ahead. This pervasive sense of defectiveness and hopelessness is
constantly reinforced by the vivid, relived memories and their body's intense threat responses.

In a relentless effort to manage this internal turmoil, Kai has adopted several maladaptive
strategies. They deliberately stay up until the early hours to delay sleep and the nightmares it
brings. Their mind is kept in a state of constant, busy distraction to crowd out intrusive thoughts.
They also systematically avoid crowded places, finding the press of unpredictable bodies unbearable.
While these strategies offer momentary relief, they perpetuate a cycle of isolation and exhaustion,
leaving Kai feeling trapped by their past and hopeless for their future.
