color his experience of the present; they intensify the sense of threat he feels during an argument
or in a crowd, interpreting these situations as proof of a hostile environment. Furthermore, his
belief in his own weakness makes the intrusive, snapshot memories feel like a personal failing, a
sign that his mind is not strong enough to contain the past.

The overwhelming nature of these threat responses—the flashbacks, the strong negative emotions, and
the physical reactivity—compels him to adopt rigid strategies for safety. He meticulously avoids the
neighborhood where his friend died. In conversations, he looks down and steers discussions away from

particularly those that feel overstimulating.

Consequently, Mr. Khan lives a life of profound isolation. His avoidance of triggers and locations,
