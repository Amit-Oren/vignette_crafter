**Clinical Case Vignette: Martin, 65-year-old European Male, Forensic Analyst**

---

**Presenting Complaints and Trauma Account**

Martin is a 65-year-old forensic analyst presenting with a PCL-5 score of 54 following a rape that
occurred during what he describes as an ordinary evening commute. He had been cycling home at dusk
when he was attacked, overpowered, and sexually assaulted. The assault occurred at a specific time
in the early evening, and his bicycle — his habitual means of travel — was present throughout. The
perpetrator was someone he had encountered briefly before, making the violation of familiarity
particularly acute. At the moment of peak danger, Martin was physically restrained and unable to
act.

**Memory Phenomenology**

Martin does not recount the assault as a continuous narrative. When asked to describe what happened,
he falls silent and then produces isolated images: the handlebars of his bicycle at an odd angle, a
particular quality of fading light at that hour, the texture of the ground beneath him. He cannot
sequence these moments into a coherent account even privately. When these images surface —
particularly during his working day, where he examines forensic evidence involving violent crime — a
surge of intense rage arrives before any conscious thought, leaving him gripping the edge of his
desk until it passes. These fragmented, rageful recollections have led to his conviction that normal
emotional life is permanently beyond him, deepening his sense of irreversible loss. That same
incoherent, affect-laden memory drives him toward desperate attempts to suppress and erase what he
holds — efforts that consume significant mental energy across his working hours.

**Central Stuck Point**

Martin's organising belief, expressed in his own words across sessions, is that *"you can never
really know what's coming — the moment you think you're safe, you're not."* He speaks of having lost
his footing in his neighbourhood, his professional networks, and among colleagues he once socialised
with. More quietly, he states that something in him has gone permanently flat — that the warmth he
once felt toward his partner now seems inaccessible, as though sealed off behind glass. His
conviction that he is permanently emotionally damaged keeps the fragmented images of the assault
close to the surface, meaning that ordinary moments of reflection are quickly overtaken by intrusive
recall. These appraisals of irreversible loss and unpredictable danger have led to his relentless
scanning of every environment he enters.

**Threat Monitoring and Avoidance**

Before entering any room at work, Martin identifies the exits. He frequently checks behind him as he
walks through the building's corridors. On public transport he positions himself with his back to a
wall. He no longer cycles. He avoids any route, object, or reference that recalls the perpetrator.
When a colleague approaches him from behind in the open-plan forensic lab, he wheels around sharply.
His elevated state of watchfulness — the constant scanning and checking — keeps these same
environmental cues active as sources of alarm, so that the bicycle outside the building and the
fading afternoon light remain potent rather than receding into background.

**Maintaining Cycle**

The dominant maintaining cycle runs through appraisal, memory, and threat. Martin's belief that the
world is permanently unpredictable means that specific triggers — the time of day, a passing
cyclist, anyone drawing emotionally close — produce immediate threat responses: scanning, exit-
planning, bodily bracing. Encountering these cues directly causes fragmented images of the assault
to surface, leaving Martin flooded with rage before he can orient himself. That rageful, incoherent
recall reinforces his appraisal of permanent emotional damage, which drives further scanning and
avoidance. His partner has noted that any attempt at emotional closeness — a hand on his shoulder, a
direct question about how he is — causes him to withdraw abruptly. Martin does not explain this to
her. The distance between them has widened steadily, and his sense of lost community and severed
connection has deepened alongside it.
